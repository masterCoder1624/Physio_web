Understanding Scoliosis & Spine Deformities

Specialized Conservative Management

Scoliosis is more than just a curve; it's a three-dimensional spinal deformity that requires specialized intervention. Whether it's Adolescent Idiopathic Scoliosis (AIS), Congenital, or Adult Degenerative curves, early conservative management is critical to controlling progression and avoiding surgery.

Our specialized physiotherapy helps by:

  • Stabilizing spinal curves through corrective exercises.
  • Improving postural awareness and respiratory function.
  • Reducing asymmetric muscle tension and pain.

Early Intervention: The goal is to maximize spinal mobility and optimize clinical outcomes before skeletal maturity, ensuring long-term health.

Common Associated Conditions

Secondary symptoms frequently managed in scoliosis and spine deformity patients

Back Pain

Clinical Symptoms

Persistent lower or upper back pain, stiffness, and difficulty maintaining posture or bending.

Therapeutic Benefits

Manual therapy combined with core stabilization to address the root cause and ensure long-term relief.

Neck Pain

Clinical Symptoms

Chronic stiffness, radiating discomfort to shoulders, and tension headaches.

Therapeutic Benefits

Cervical mobilization and ergonomic rehabilitation to restore mobility and eliminate pain.

Slip Disc (Herniated Disc)

Clinical Symptoms

Sharp radiating pain, numbness, and limb weakness due to disc compression.

Therapeutic Benefits

Non-surgical spinal decompression and traction therapy to promote natural disc healing.

Sciatica

Clinical Symptoms

Radiating nerve pain from the lower back through the legs, often with burning sensations.

Therapeutic Benefits

Nerve mobilization and corrective stretching to relieve compression and restore function.
